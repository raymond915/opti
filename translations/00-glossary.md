## Notes for reviewers

1. **Tone:** Use formal/professional Afrikaans (`u`-form when addressing the visitor where appropriate; `jy`-form is fine for direct CTAs like "Bespreek jou gratis konsultasie" — this is increasingly the SA business-Afrikaans norm).
2. **Numbers and currency:** R7,500 stays "R7 500" (Afrikaans uses non-breaking space as thousands separator, no comma). Dates in Afrikaans format: "29 Maart 2027" not "29 March 2027".
3. **Acronyms on first mention** in body copy: spell out in Afrikaans then put English acronym in brackets. After that, use the acronym alone.
4. **British English vs Afrikaans spelling:** The site uses South African English (organisation, organisation, behaviour). Afrikaans equivalents are generally what you'd find in modern Afrikaans newspapers (Beeld, Rapport, Volksblad).
5. **Legal page content** (POPIA, Terms of Use) should be reviewed by a qualified Afrikaans legal translator before publication. Auto-translation here carries legal risk.
