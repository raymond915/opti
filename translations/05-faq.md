| **Q:** Which labour laws apply to my business? | **V:** Watter arbeidswette geld vir my onderneming? |
| **A:** All South African employers are subject to the Basic Conditions of Employment Act (BCEA), the Labour Relations Act (LRA), and the Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA). All businesses must comply with POPIA for employee data. | **A:** Alle Suid-Afrikaanse werkgewers is onderhewig aan die Wet op Basiese Diensvoorwaardes (BCEA), die Wet op Arbeidsverhoudinge (LRA) en die Wet op Beroepsgesondheid en - veiligheid (OHSA). Aangewese werkgewers (50 of meer werknemers) moet ook voldoen aan die Wet op Gelyke Indiensneming (EEA). Alle ondernemings moet voldoen aan POPIA vir werknemersdata. |

| **Q:** What is the Section 189 process? | **V:** Wat is die Artikel 189-proses? |
| **A:** Section 189 of the Labour Relations Act requires employers contemplating retrenchments to consult with affected employees before any dismissals take effect. The consultation must cover the reasons for proposed retrenchments, alternatives considered, proposed selection criteria, and severance pay. Failure to follow this process results in procedurally unfair dismissal findings. | **A:** Artikel 189 van die Wet op Arbeidsverhoudinge vereis dat werkgewers wat afleggings oorweeg, met geaffekteerde werknemers moet konsulteer voordat enige ontslae van krag word. Die konsultasie moet die redes vir voorgestelde afleggings, alternatiewe wat oorweeg is, voorgestelde keurkriteria en skeidingspakkette dek. Versuim om hierdie proses te volg, lei tot bevindings van prosedureel onbillike ontslag. |

## Reviewer notes for FAQ page

- Acronyms like CCMA, BCEA, LRA, OHSA, EEA, POPIA — kept identical to English (these are statutory names recognised in both languages).
- **SARO vs SACE** — there's a translation choice. SACE is the official acronym used by the Council itself in both languages. Some Afrikaans writers use SARO. The FAQ uses both for the first mention then SACE going forward — feel free to standardise.
- **"Aangewese werkgewers"** — official Afrikaans term for "designated employers" (legal definition under EEA).
- Long answers retain paragraph breaks — when implementing in JSON, use `\n\n` between paragraphs.
